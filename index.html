Rules & field notes
  1. Shade exactly N cells as firebreaks. The ★ and the numbered cells can never be shaded.
  2. Fire starts on the ★ at minute 0. Each minute it spreads from every burning cell to every orthogonally adjacent unshaded cell.
  3. Every unshaded cell must eventually burn.
  4. Every numbered cell must catch fire at exactly the minute shown.

Field notes

  • A cell’s burn minute is the length of the shortest unshaded route from the ★ — never less than its straight-line distance. A big number means the fire was forced around something.
  • Unshaded neighbors burn within a minute of each other, and a cell burning at minute t has a neighbor that burned at t−1.
  • The dot mark is a pencil note for ground you’ve proven must stay open. It isn’t checked.

Every fire is generated on the spot and machine-verified: exactly one valid placement of breaks, and a solving path that needs no guessing — only deduction.
